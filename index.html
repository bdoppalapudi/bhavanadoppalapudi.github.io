About Me

Researcher. Builder. Problem Solver.

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of South Florida, specializing in Large Language Models, Generative AI, and Natural Language Processing. My research focuses on building reliable, scalable AI systems that bridge the gap between academic rigor and real-world deployment.

My doctoral work centers on GenFakeNewsNet — a four-agent LLM orchestration system for synthetic disinformation benchmark generation, producing over 17,000 article pairs evaluated through large-scale human studies. This work has been published in IEEE and Springer venues.

Beyond research, I bring hands-on industry experience through ML internships at Amadeus IT Group and Florida Blue, where I developed production-grade forecasting and classification systems.

Springer · ISVC 2024 First Author

Seeing Is Believing: The Role of Scatterplots in Recommender System Trust and Decision-Making

Bhavana Doppalapudi, Md Dilshadur Rahman, Paul Rosen · International Symposium on Visual Computing. Springer, Cham.

Investigated the impact of scatterplot visualizations on trust and decision-making in recommender systems through a two-part human-subject experiment. Results show that descriptive accuracy labels (high/medium/low) foster greater trust than numeric accuracy values, with significant implications for human-AI interaction design.
